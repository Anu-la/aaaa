Poetry

There once was a girl,
She breathed poetry, and drowned in misery,
All she spoke was words profound, tortured by the meanings unfound,
But deep inside, was a world - not so bright
Deeper than there, was a girl masked and un-named,
Who's misery, was not the sentences unturned,
It wasn't the girl upfront,
It wasn't the words undone, but-
There were poetries scattered-
Sentences shuffled and feelings jumbled,
That- was the misery of the girl well hidden,
The girl long forgotten;
While the one upfront,
Drowned in misery, of poetry that resembled the girl long gone;
